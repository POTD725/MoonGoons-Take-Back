# Licensing Scope

The repository’s source code and project documentation are released under the MIT License in the root `LICENSE` file.

## What the MIT License covers here

Unless a file states otherwise, the MIT License applies to original source code, Godot scenes, JSON configuration, tests, shell scripts, and written documentation committed to this repository.

## What it does not automatically cover

The MIT License does not automatically grant rights to use content that is separately licensed or reserved, including:

- The **MoonGoons**, **MoonGoons: Take Back**, and **MoonGoons: Crime Wars** names, logos, and brand identity.
- Third-party art, audio, fonts, plugins, SDKs, asset packs, and trademarks.
- Assets or files marked with another license, attribution requirement, or all-rights-reserved notice.
- Private credentials, deployment configuration, player data, or exported store builds.

Every external package or asset must be listed in `docs/CREDITS.md` with its source, license, required attribution, and intended use before a public release.

## Contributor rule

By submitting a pull request, a contributor represents that they have the right to submit their contribution and agrees that the contribution may be distributed under the repository’s MIT License unless its files explicitly declare another compatible license.

## Release checklist

Before publishing a build:

1. Review every third-party dependency and asset license.
2. Add required notices to `docs/CREDITS.md` and in-game credits.
3. Confirm that store, platform, and export terms are compatible with each dependency.
4. Seek qualified legal advice for commercial licensing, trademark registration, contributor agreements, privacy, or monetization questions.
